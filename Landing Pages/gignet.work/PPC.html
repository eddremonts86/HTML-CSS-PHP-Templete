Google Ads

Google is the biggest search engine in the world and provides many options for the marketeer. With Google Ads you have the opportunity to have you ads shown on different platforms: Google Search, Youtube and Google Display which covers more than 2 million sites throughout the internet including Gmail. These different platforms covers both push and pull marketing and offers the marketeer different options when carrying out an online strategy. The different advantages of the platforms makes it possible to tailor ads to potential customers with content that is specific to where in the buying process the customer is. Is the customer just browsing for options, ready to purchase or can we re-engage them and make them purchase again.

Google Search

Google Search covers almost the entire market and is responsible for 93% of the search engine market share worldwide.Google Search will show ads that matches the keyword a customer has typed in. This is a great opportunity to reach potential customers as they are actively searching for information, products etc, and is thus asking to be exposed to information. You only pay when someone clicks you ad. Targeting options includes keywords, different demographics, geography, time of day and interests and any combination of these

Youtube

Youtube allows you to show videos to potential customers. The length of videos can vary from 5 second to unlimited. Ads are banners on videos, alongside suggested videos, or video ads before the content. The standard is video ads that can't be skipped until 5 seconds in, but can also be unskippable 5 second video ads. Advertiser is charged if someone clicks the ad, watches it in its entirety, or 30 seconds, whichever comes first. Targeting criteria includes search queries, placements (channels/videos), interests, demographics, geographic and time, and any combination of these.

Bing Ads

Bing ads accounts for 2.4% market share worldwide, however, CPC prices are often cheaper on Bing than on Google. Bing is thus a nice supplement to Google Search in order to cover the entire market as much as possible.
